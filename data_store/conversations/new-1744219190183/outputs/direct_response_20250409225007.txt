The capital of India is New Delhi.

New Delhi is a city and a union territory of India, officially known as the National Capital Territory (NCT) of Delhi. It serves as the seat of the government of India. It is located within the megacity of Delhi and is a part of the larger Delhi metropolitan area.

Here are some key facts about New Delhi:

*   **Historical Significance**: New Delhi has a rich history, with evidence of settlement dating back thousands of years. It has been the site of several historical cities and empires.

*   **Modern Capital**: The foundation stone of New Delhi was laid in 1911, and it was officially inaugurated as the capital of India in 1931. It was designed by British architects Sir Edwin Lutyens and Sir Herbert Baker.

*   **Administrative Center**: New Delhi houses the executive, legislative, and judiciary branches of the Government of India, including the Rashtrapati Bhavan (Presidential Palace), Parliament House, and the Supreme Court.

*   **Cultural and Economic Hub**: New Delhi is a major cultural, commercial, and economic center. It has numerous historical monuments, museums, gardens, and cultural institutions.

*   **Geography**: New Delhi is located in the northern part of India and is part of the Indo-Gangetic Plain.

*   **Demographics**: As part of the larger Delhi metropolitan area, New Delhi is densely populated and is one of the largest urban areas in the world.

In summary, New Delhi is the capital of India, serving as the center of government, culture, and commerce, with a rich historical background and modern infrastructure.
